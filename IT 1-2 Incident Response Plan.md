# Privacy and Security Incident Response Plan

## Purpose

|||||||| is committed to protecting all sensitive information relating to its members, workforce, and business operations.

This document defines a structured approach for handling actual or potential threats to information, whether stored electronically or physically. It is a living document and may be updated as processes improve.

---

## Scope

This plan applies to all data owned or managed by ||||||||, including:
- Internal systems and records
- Data handled by third parties or vendors
- Any environment where sensitive data is stored or transmitted

---

## Goals of Incident Response

In the event of a privacy or security incident, |||||||| aims to:

1. Investigate the incident internally (and with law enforcement if required)
2. Mitigate harm to affected individuals
3. Minimize operational, reputational, and financial impact
4. Communicate appropriately:
   - To affected individuals
   - To regulatory bodies or media (if required)
   - To internal staff and leadership
5. Support corrective actions and disciplinary measures where necessary
6. Conduct post-incident review and training to prevent recurrence

---

## Definitions

### Privacy Incident
Any unauthorized:
- Access
- Disclosure
- Use
- Modification
- Destruction

of sensitive data that compromises confidentiality, integrity, or availability.

### Security Incident
Any known or suspected event that may place sensitive data at risk.

---

## Incident Response Team

Team composition depends on incident scope and may include:

- Compliance Officer
- Security Officer
- Privacy and Security Analyst
- Legal
- Senior Leadership
- Risk Management
- IT Department
- Public Relations
- Third-party vendors

---

## Incident Lifecycle

### Phases

#### Discovery / Reporting
- Incident identified or reported
- Initial involvement of management, IT, and compliance

#### Immediate Response (0–1 Business Day)
- Containment
- Case creation
- Escalation
- Team activation

#### Continuing Response (0–15+ Days)
- Analysis and planning
- Investigation
- Mitigation and correction
- Notification
- Reporting and closure

---

## Reporting an Incident

Incidents may be reported through:

- Employees or contractors
- Management
- IT tickets
- Email systems
- Compliance channels
- Third-party vendors
- Hotline systems

### Examples of Reportable Incidents

- Unauthorized access or disclosure of information
- Loss or theft of devices or data
- Network intrusions
- Misuse of systems
- Data transmission errors
- Physical record exposure

---

## Determination of Incident

The Compliance Officer (or designee) determines whether an incident meets response criteria.

If not, findings are documented and the case is closed.

---

## Initial Response

### Containment

Actions may include:

- Disconnecting systems
- Securing physical records
- Halting processes
- Restricting access

### Case Documentation

- All actions must be documented
- Documentation must support legal, regulatory, and internal review

### Escalation

Escalation depends on:

- Risk to individuals
- Legal reporting requirements
- Operational impact
- Involvement of third parties

---

## Continuing Response

### Analysis and Planning

- Assess scope and impact
- Identify legal obligations
- Develop response plan
- Coordinate communications

### Investigation

- Conduct forensic analysis if needed
- Interview involved parties
- Document findings thoroughly

### Mitigation

Examples include:

- Credit monitoring services
- Legal action
- Vendor contract review
- Security enhancements

### Correction

- Update policies and procedures
- Improve systems and controls
- Conduct training
- Apply disciplinary action if needed

---

## Notification

The Incident Response Team determines:

- Who must be notified
- When notification must occur
- How notification is delivered

This may include:

- Affected individuals
- Regulatory agencies
- Media (if required)

---

## Case Closure

Before closing:

- All response goals must be met
- Documentation must be complete
- Lessons learned must be recorded

---

## Reporting

|||||||| will comply with all legal reporting requirements.

- Regulatory notifications will be completed within required timelines
- Large-scale incidents may require media coordination
- Internal reporting will support organizational improvement

---

## Appendix A – Enterprise Systems

### Internet / Connectivity
- Diagnose whether issue is isolated or widespread
- Escalate to ISP or infrastructure provider as needed

### VPN
- Used for secure access
- Issues typically user-specific unless system-wide outage

### DNS
- Internal and external systems
- Failover and reconfiguration required if multiple nodes fail

### Active Directory
- Hybrid system with cloud backup
- Failures trigger automated alerts

### Virtual Infrastructure
- Backups maintained via virtualization platforms
- Disaster recovery available via snapshots and restore systems

### Backup Systems
- Multiple layers of redundancy
- Cloud and physical backups available

---

## Appendix B – Tier 1 Systems

### Emergency Communications Systems
- Phone systems
- Dispatch systems
- Emergency alert systems

**General Response:**
- Switch to backup systems
- Use manual processes if needed
- Coordinate with IT and vendors

---

## Appendix C – Tier 2 Systems

## Appendix D – Tier 3 Systems

- Contact vendor support
